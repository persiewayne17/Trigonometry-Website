What is Trigonometry?

Trigonometry is one of the most important branches of mathematics that finds huge application in diverse fields. The branch called “Trigonometry” basically deals with the study of the relationship between the sides and angles of a right-angle triangle. Hence, it helps to find the missing or unknown angles or sides of a right triangle using the trigonometric formulas, functions, or trigonometric identities.

In trigonometry, the angles can be either measured in degrees or radians. Some of the most commonly used trigonometric angles for calculations are 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, and 90°.

  • Trigonometry Ratios and Rules: Understanding the basic trigonometric ratios (sine, cosine, tangent) and their relationships.
  • Basic Trigonometric Ratios and Their Reciprocals: Learning about the reciprocals of sine (cosecant), cosine (secant), and tangent (cotangent).
  • Angles in Radian: Converting angles from degrees to radians and understanding radian measure.
  • Sine and Cosine: Exploring the sine and cosine functions, their properties, and their applications.
  • Compound and Multiple Angles: Dealing with formulas and identities involving compound angles (e.g., sin(A + B), cos(A + B)) and multiple angles (e.g., sin(2A), cos(2A)).
  • Trigonometry Functions: Analyzing the main trigonometric functions, including sine, cosine, tangent, and their properties.
  • Graphs of Trigonometry Functions: Plotting and interpreting the graphs of trigonometric functions.
  • Solving Trigonometry Equations: Techniques for solving equations that involve trigonometric functions.
  • Maximum and Minimum Points of Trigonometric Functions: Identifying and understanding the extreme values of trigonometric functions.

What are the six basic Trigonometric Functions?

There are 6 trigonometric functions which are:

  • Sine Function
  • Cosine Function
  • Tan Function
  • Sec Function
  • Cot Function
  • Cosec function

What is the formula for six trigonometry functions?

The formula for six trigonometry functions are:

  • Sine A = Opposite side/Hypotenuse
  • Cosine A = Adjacent side / Hypotenuse
  • Tan A = Opposite side / Adjacent side
  • Sec A = Hypotenuse / Adjacent side
  • Cot A = Adjacent side / Opposite side
  • Cosec A = Hypotenuse / Opposite side

What is the primary function of trigonometry?

The three primary functions of trigonometry are Sine function, Cosine function and Tangent Function.

Who is the founder of trigonometry?

A greek astronomer, geographer and mathematician, Hipparchus discovered the concept of trigonometry.
