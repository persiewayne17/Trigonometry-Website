Trigonometry Examples: Questions and Answers

Question 1:

Solve, two friends, Rakesh and Vishal started climbing a pyramid-shaped hill. Rakesh climbs 315 m and finds that the angle of depression is 72.3 degrees from his starting point. How high is he from the ground?

Answer:

Let m be the height above the ground.

To find: Value of m

To solve m, use the sine ratio.

sin 72.3° = m/315

0.953 = m/315

m = 315 × 0.953

m = 300.195 m
The man is 300.195 m above the ground.

Question 2:

Solve, A man is observing a pole of height 55 feet. According to his measurement, the pole casts a 23-foot long shadow. Can you help him to know the angle of elevation of the sun from the tip of the shadow?

Answer:

Let x be the angle of elevation of the sun.

tan x = 55/23 = 2.391

x = tan-1(2.391)

x = 67.30°
