Compound and Multiple Angles

In trigonometry, compound and multiple angles refer to specific formulas that help us find the sine, cosine, and tangent of the sum, difference, or multiples of angles. These formulas are very useful when working with trigonometric identities and solving trigonometric equations.

1. Compound Angles

Compound angles involve the addition or subtraction of two angles. The most common formulas are:

  • Sine of a Sum or Difference:

sin(A ± B) = sin A cos B ± cos A sin B

Example: If A = 30° and B = 45°, then

sin(30° + 45°) = sin 30° cos 45° + cos 30° sin 45°

Using the values sin 30° = 1/2, cos 45° = √2/2, cos 30° = √3/2, and sin 45° = √2/2, we get:

sin 75° = (1/2) × (√2/2) + (√3/2) × (√2/2) = (√2/4) + (√6/4) = (√2 + √6)/4

  • Cosine of a Sum or Difference:

cos(A ± B) = cos A cos B ∓ sin A sin B

Example: If A = 60° and B = 30°, then

cos(60° - 30°) = cos 60° cos 30° + sin 60° sin 30°

Using the values cos 60° = 1/2, cos 30° = √3/2, sin 60° = √3/2, and sin 30° = 1/2, we get:

cos 30° = (1/2) × (√3/2) + (√3/2) × (1/2) = (√3/4) + (√3/4) = √3/2

  • Tangent of a Sum or Difference:

tan(A ± B) = (tan A ± tan B) / (1 ∓ tan A tan B)

Example: If A = 45° and B = 15°, then

tan(45° + 15°) = (tan 45° + tan 15°) / (1 - tan 45° × tan 15°)

Using tan 45° = 1 and tan 15° ≈ 0.268, we get:

tan 60° ≈ (1 + 0.268) / (1 - 1 × 0.268) = 1.268 / 0.732 ≈ 1.732

2. Multiple Angles

Multiple angles involve finding the sine, cosine, or tangent of an angle that is multiplied by an integer, like 2A or 3A. The most common formulas are:

  • Double Angle Formulas:

Sine: sin(2A) = 2 sin A cos A

Example: If A = 30°, then

sin(2 × 30°) = 2 sin 30° cos 30° = 2 × (1/2) × (√3/2) = √3/2

Cosine: cos(2A) = cos² A - sin² A

Example: If A = 45°, then

cos(2 × 45°) = cos² 45° - sin² 45° = (√2/2)² - (√2/2)² = 0

Tangent: tan(2A) = (2 tan A) / (1 - tan² A)

Example: If A = 30°, then

tan(2 × 30°) = (2 × tan 30°) / (1 - tan² 30°) = (2 × 1/√3) / (1 - (1/√3)²) = √3

These formulas are essential in solving more complex trigonometric equations and proving trigonometric identities. They also help in solving problems involving angles in geometry and physics.
