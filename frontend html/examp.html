Trigonometry Examples: Questions and Answers

Question 1:

Solve, two friends, Rakesh and Vishal started climbing a pyramid-shaped hill. Rakesh climbs 315 m and finds that the angle of depression is 72.3 degrees from his starting point. How high is he from the ground?.

Answer:

Let m is the height above the ground.

To find: Value of m

To solve m, use the sine ratio.

sin 72.3° = m/315

0.953= m/315

m = 315 x 0.953

m = 300.195 mtr
The man is 300.195 mtr above the ground.

Question 2:

Solve, A man is observing a pole of height 55 foot. According to his measurement, pole cast a 23 feet long shadow. Can you help him to know the angle of elevation of the sun from the tip of shadow?

Answer:

Let x be the angles of elevation of the sun, then

tan x = 55/23 = 2.391

x = tan-1(2.391)

x = 67.30°

Question 3:

Find the length of the hypotenuse of a right triangle if one of the angles is 30° and the length of the side opposite this angle is 5 cm.

Answer:

We use the sine function, which is defined as:

sin(θ) = Opposite / Hypotenuse

Here, θ = 30° and Opposite = 5 cm.

So, sin(30°) = 5 / Hypotenuse

We know that sin(30°) = 0.5. Therefore,

0.5 = 5 / Hypotenuse

Hypotenuse = 5 / 0.5 = 10 cm

Final Answer: The length of the hypotenuse is 10 cm.

Question 4:

Determine the value of angle θ in a right triangle if the length of the adjacent side is 4 cm and the hypotenuse is 8 cm.

Answer:

We use the cosine function, which is defined as:

cos(θ) = Adjacent / Hypotenuse

Here, Adjacent = 4 cm and Hypotenuse = 8 cm.

So, cos(θ) = 4 / 8 = 0.5

We know that cos(60°) = 0.5. Therefore,

θ = 60°

Final Answer: The value of angle θ is 60°.

Question 5:

In a right triangle, if the opposite side is 7 cm and the adjacent side is 24 cm, find the value of the angle θ.

Answer:

We use the tangent function, which is defined as:

tan(θ) = Opposite / Adjacent

Here, Opposite = 7 cm and Adjacent = 24 cm.

So, tan(θ) = 7 / 24

Using a calculator, we find:

θ = tan-1(7 / 24) ≈ 16.26°

Final Answer: The value of angle θ is approximately 16.26°.

Question 6:

If sin(θ) = 0.866 and θ is an acute angle, what is the value of θ?

Answer:

We use the inverse sine function:

θ = sin-1(0.866)

Using a calculator, we find:

θ ≈ 60°

Final Answer: The value of θ is approximately 60°.
